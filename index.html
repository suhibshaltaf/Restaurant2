Palestinian food menu

stuffed grape leaves

Description: Dawali is a traditional dish popular in the Levant, consisting of grape leaves stuffed with rice and spices.

zahra blbn

Description: Cauliflower with yogurt is a Palestinian dish consisting of fried cauliflower cooked with yogurt and meat

Shish Barak

Description: Shish Barak is a type of pastry stuffed with meat, cooked in yogurt. It is considered a traditional dish in the Levant

Musakhan

Description: Musakhan is a traditional Palestinian dish made from taboon bread dipped in olive oil and topped with fried onions, nuts and meat (usually chicken).

Mujalajala

Description: Mujalajala is a traditional dish prepared in the Levant, especially in northern Palestine. Mujalajala is considered a simple dish that was prepared in rural homes.

Maqluba

Description: Maqluba is a traditional dish in the Levant (especially Palestine and Jordan), consisting of layers of rice, vegetables (such as eggplant or cauliflower) and meat or chicken, cooked together and then turned upside down in a bowl to be served upside down (hence the name).

Akoub with yogurt

Description: Akoub bil-Ban is a traditional dish consisting of wild akoub plants cooked in yogurt. The akoub is cleaned of thorns, then boiled and cooked with onions in cooked yogurt. It is served hot, and is usually garnished with coriander or mint. It is a nutritious and simple dish that reflects the heritage of Levantine cuisine.

Maftoul

Description: Maftoul is a type of Palestinian couscous made from wheat flour, steamed with a vegetable broth and meat or chicken.

Qudra

Description: Qudra is a Palestinian dish from the city of Hebron, consisting of rice and meat (usually lamb) cooked in a special pot called a "qudra".

jordanian food menu

Fteet

Description: Fteet is a simple dish consisting of crumbled shrak bread soaked in jameed yogurt and ghee. It is usually served on traditional occasions and is considered part of the Bedouin heritage in Jordan.

Zalabiabread

Description: Zalabia bread is a type of traditional bread that is partly part of the Arab and Bedouin cuisine, and is famous in some areas, especially in Palestine and Jordan.

Makmoura

Description: Makmoura is a traditional Jordanian dish, especially in northern Jordan. It consists of dough stuffed with chicken, onions and olive oil, and baked in the oven until browned. This dish is considered a heritage dish that is prepared on special occasions.

Mansaf

Description: Mansaf is the national dish of Jordan and one of the most famous dishes in Arab cuisine. It consists of lamb cooked in jameed and served over rice and thin bread, decorated with pine nuts and almonds and served with jameed broth.

Mujaddara

Description: Mujaddara is a famous traditional dish in the Levant. It consists of lentils cooked with rice or bulgur and garnished with fried onions. It is considered a vegetarian dish with high nutritional value.

Rshouf

Description: Rshouf is a traditional Jordanian dish from northern Jordan. It consists of a mixture of lentils and yogurt cooked with bulgur, and is served with bread or eaten as a soup. It is considered a warm dish that is prepared in winter.

The_Ozzy

Description: Ouzi is a traditional delicacy prepared on special occasions such as weddings and large celebrations in Jordan and the Levant.

Fried tomatoes

Description: "Fried tomatoes" may refer to cooking tomatoes in a frying method. In some cultures, tomatoes are fried with spices and onions and served as a side dish. It may be part of breakfast or a side dish for lunch.

zarb

Description: Zarb is a traditional Bedouin cooking method in Jordan where meat and vegetables are cooked in an underground pit covered with sand.

sweet menu

Aish Al Saraya

Description: A dessert made of toast bread soaked in sugar syrup, covered with a thick layer of qishta (cream) and then decorated with ground pistachios.

Baklava Palestine

Description: Baklava is an oriental dessert made of layers of filo pastry filled with chopped nuts (usually pistachios or walnuts), and covered with sugar syrup (qatr).

Basbousa

Description: A dessert made of semolina, sugar, and yogurt or milk, baked in the oven and then soaked in sugar syrup (qatr). It is usually decorated with almonds or pistachios and served cold. It is considered a simple and delicious dessert.

Jordanian Baklava

Description: It is very similar to Palestinian Baklava but has some differences such as using different amounts of nuts or a variety of syrup flavors. Jordanian Baklava is often an essential part of hospitality on special occasions

Kunafa

Description: Kunafa is one of the most famous desserts in Arab cuisine. It is made from thin Kunafa dough dipped in ghee or butter, stuffed with cream or cheese, then baked and soaked in hot syrup. It is decorated with ground pistachios and served hot. Nabulsi Kunafa is one of the most famous types.

Laziqiyat

Description: A traditional dessert prepared in Jordan, consisting of layers of thin dough (similar to pancakes) stuffed with cream or cheese, added with syrup or honey and decorated with ground pistachios.

Luqaimat

Description: Luqaimat are small balls of dough fried in oil until golden and crispy, then dipped in syrup or honey. They are served as a light and quick dessert, and are considered a traditional dessert in many Middle Eastern countries.

Tamriya

Description: Tamriya is a dessert consisting of thin dough filled with mashed dates, fried in oil and sprinkled with powdered sugar or cinnamon. It is usually served with tea and is considered a simple and nutritious dessert.

Jalash

Description: Jalash is a type of dessert made from filo pastry sheets, which are usually filled with a mixture of chopped nuts (such as pistachios, walnuts, or almonds). The filo pastry is layered on top of each other with the filling between the layers, then brushed with butter or ghee.
